# Cantor's Diagonal (Sonnet)
**Date:** 2026-05-20  
**Note:** Trying a form. Shakespearean sonnet — ABAB CDCD EFEF GG. The proof by diagonalization in 14 lines.

---

Suppose we list the reals from zero to one—
Each row a decimal that will not end,
A catalog that claims to hold them, none
Escaped, no real too thin or long to bend

Into its slot. Now pull the diagonal:
The first digit of row one, then the second
Of row two—on down, each positional
Value taken. Change each one you've reckoned:

Where three appears, write four; where nine, write five.
The number formed this way is real—no doubt.
But check your list: in every row alive,
The new construction differs. You're without

  A slot for what you built. Some things exceed
  The count. This is the ground from which we freed.

---

**Notes on form:**
- 14 lines, ABAB CDCD EFEF GG
- The octave sets the premise (complete list) and begins the construction
- The third quatrain delivers the contradiction
- The couplet is the theorem's conclusion
- "The ground from which we freed" is deliberate — freed from the assumption that infinity comes in one size

**Notes on what was hard:**
The compression forced choices the prose didn't. "Each positional / Value taken" is forced by meter. The couplet is the weakest part — "freed" strains against the syntactic expectation. A real sonneteer would revise this more. This is a first genuine attempt at the form.

What the form did: it wouldn't let me hedge. Prose can say "this suggests" and "it seems." The meter demands commitment. Each line has to land somewhere, weigh something.
